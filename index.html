The Challenge

Traditional neurofeedback requires 20–40 in‑clinic sessions with a practitioner, driving cost and scheduling burden. Retention in existing solutions is low, with high dropout rates, limiting therapeutic impact. A full course can cost $4,000–$8,000, often not covered by insurance.

Our Solution

MindfulNest combines a consumer EEG headband (Muse S Athena) with an AI-driven adaptive engine that emulates key functions of a trained neurofeedback practitioner, delivering personalized feedback in real time through game mechanics. Children play mini-games while passively receiving neurofeedback; dynamic on-screen cues (e.g., vignette shading) map instantaneous brain state changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What hardware does MindfulNest use?

The system uses the Muse S Athena EEG headband (dry fabric electrodes, multi-channel frontal/temporal coverage, IMU). Its low-friction, at-home form factor allows our clients to self-calibrate quickly while we run automatic signal quality and artifact checks before and during sessions.

How is the experience personalized?

NeurOS's LLM layer selects and adjusts macro protocol parameters (target bands, reward contingencies, pacing) based on longitudinal session summaries and parent symptom inputs. A lower-level ML pipeline continuously adapts per-session thresholds (e.g., adaptive reward gating, artifact rejection parameters) using rolling baselines and recent distributional shifts in the child's EEG features.

What outcomes do you track?

We collect multi-dimensional data: caregiver-rated symptom instruments (e.g., ATEC domains), objective attention / executive function tasks, and EEG spectral + connectivity metrics (e.g., theta/beta ratio, coherence / hyperconnectivity indicators). Parent and teacher qualitative notes plus in-game engagement/compliance metrics (session completion, reward stability) are integrated to contextualize neurophysiological changes; aggregated trends guide subsequent protocol adjustments.
